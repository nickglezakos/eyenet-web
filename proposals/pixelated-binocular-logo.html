Pixelated Binocular Logo — Design Proposal

Twin circular eye-rings (no V connector), built from rects, inspired by Candidate 2.

0. Reference — Candidate 2

Original (with V connector)

Key elements extracted from the reference — without the V:

  • Two circular eye-rings side by side (binocular / owl-eye motif).
  • Thick ring wall — the circles are not thin outlines; the ring is ≈40 % of the radius.
  • Dark hollow centre (pupil) — transparent, showing the background.
  • Two horizontal scan lines crossing through the pupil area, evoking data / surveillance.
  • Single bright cyan hue with a glow — in our pixelated version this becomes the Eyenet 3-blue palette with opacity modulation.

1. Design DNA

Same construction rules as the Eyenet wordmark: axis-aligned <rect> elements only, three cycling blues, opacity modulation 0.65 – 1.0.

2. Proposed Concepts

Five concepts at different fidelity levels — all built from the same DNA, all using only <rect> elements, the 3-blue palette, and opacity modulation. The V connector from the reference is intentionally removed. Concepts D and E are robot/antenna character variants of the chunky style.

Concept A — "Twin Eyes" Recommended

Two pixelated circular eye-rings on a 26 × 12 cell grid with an 8 px cell size (208 × 96 px native). Good balance of fidelity and compactness — the ring shape reads clearly, and the scan lines are prominent. ~200 rects total. Proportions: 2.17 : 1. Total rects: ~184.

A more compact variant with rounded antenna tips (2×2 blobs) above each eye and single-cell stalks. The bridge bar connects at the scan line. Evokes a friendly robot or IoT sensor. 16 × 9 cell grid, 16 px cells (256 × 144 px native). ~66 rects.

Concept F — "Antenna Bot Lockup" Complete Brand Mark

The Antenna Bot icon (Concept E) paired with the original EYENET pixelated wordmark as a unified vertical lockup. Both elements share the same rect-only construction and colour palette — the icon sits above a 24 px gap, centred over the wordmark. Ideal for splash screens, about pages, print headers, social-media profile images, and anywhere you need the full brand identity in a single mark.

5. Implementation Notes

File Naming Convention

Following the existing structure in assets/logos/:

  • svg/binocular-light.svg — Concept A, light-mode palette
  • svg/binocular-dark.svg — Concept A, dark-mode palette
  • svg/binocular-dense-light.svg — Concept B, light-mode
  • svg/binocular-dense-dark.svg — Concept B, dark-mode
  • svg/binocular-chunky-light.svg — Concept C, light-mode
  • svg/binocular-chunky-dark.svg — Concept C, dark-mode
  • svg/binocular-robot-light.svg — Concept D, light-mode
  • svg/binocular-robot-dark.svg — Concept D, dark-mode
  • svg/binocular-antenna-light.svg — Concept E, light-mode
  • svg/binocular-antenna-dark.svg — Concept E, dark-mode
  • png/binocular/light/ — PNG exports at 32, 64, 128, 192, 512 px
  • png/binocular/dark/ — same sizes
  • favicon/ — ICO and PNG from Concept C

Animation Potential

  • Scan sweep: Animate scan-line rects opacity in sequence (left→right wave).
  • Blink: Collapse ring rows vertically toward centre, then restore.
  • Pulse: Cycle ring-rect opacities in a radial wave from centre outward.
  • Focus: Expand / contract the inner void radius (toggle between concepts A and B).

Colour Theming

Every rect uses a fill from the shared palette. To re-theme, replace the base hues globally — the opacity modulation and grid structure carry the visual effect regardless of hue.
